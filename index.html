Academic AI Integrity

Academic AI integrity combines AI literacy with a focus on academic integrity: building the knowledge and skills to use AI tools effectively while maintaining ethical practices and scholarly responsibility in academic work.

Research synthesis

How students and teachers experience AI concerns

Over 600 articles have been published collecting perspectives of students and teachers on the integration of AI in education. Both groups have their concerns about whether and how to integrate AI into academic practice. This visual allows you to explore the most common trends, with an estimation of their relative concernedness based on how many articles mentioned students and/or teachers as concerned about each theme.

Despite these widespread concerns, generative AI has become part of academic life. Over 90% of students now use AI tools, yet consistent norms and a shared language remain rare across programmes. This course builds that foundation: connecting what AI is, how to use it thoughtfully, how to evaluate its outputs, and how to act with integrity. Designed for both students and teachers, it addresses the concerns shown above and culminates in two authentic artefacts: a course-ready AI rationale for teachers and an assignment-ready disclosure for students.

AI integration modes

Choosing your course's AI integration level

Teachers can choose one of five integration modes depending on learning goals. Click a wedge or card to explore each level, from No AI to AI as Learning Goal. Each mode connects directly to course design and assessment strategy.

These integration levels provide a shared vocabulary for discussing AI boundaries with students. The course helps teachers articulate their chosen level and understand its implications for assessment design.

Course design

Six principles that guide the course design

These principles shape every decision in the course, from module sequence to artefact design. Hover each numbered node to see the principle and its rationale.

Each of the six principles maps directly to a course element and a recommended activity, making it clear not just what to teach, but how to act on it.

Mapping

From principles to practice: mapping course elements

Each design principle underpins a specific element of the course. Principles 1–3 form the Skills & Literacy zone and ground the three active learning modules. Principles 4–6 form the Integrity & Responsibility zone and anchor the policy and reflective elements of the course.
